1. Executive Summary
MeetingDNA is a B2B SaaS decision intelligence engine that automatically converts
company meeting recordings and transcripts into a structured, queryable decision
knowledge graph — with zero behavior change required from any team member.
It answers the question every growing company struggles with: 'Why did we decide that,
and what else broke when we changed it?'
Every company above ten people silently suffers from institutional amnesia. Decisions get made
in meetings, reasoning stays locked in someone's head, and months later nobody can explain
why the pricing changed, who owned the infra migration, or what assumptions the product
roadmap was built on.
Existing tools like Notion and Confluence attempt to solve this through documentation — but
documentation requires discipline that teams consistently fail to maintain. MeetingDNA takes a
fundamentally different approach: it works on the recordings your team already makes, requiring
nothing new from anyone.
2. Problem Statement
2.1 The Institutional Amnesia Problem
Most organizations operate with a painful but invisible inefficiency: they have no reliable record of
why decisions were made. The consequences compound quietly across every team, every
quarter.
• Decisions get reversed without anyone knowing what downstream dependencies they
break
• New hires spend weeks reconstructing context that was never written down
• Engineering teams build on assumptions that were invalidated in meetings they never
attended
• Strategy pivots create cascading misalignments nobody tracks or surfaces

2.3 The Real Cost
This is not a minor inconvenience. Consider a Series B company of 80 people running two hours
of meetings per day. Over six months that's roughly 15,000 hours of decisions made — none of
them indexed, none of them queryable, none of them connected to each other. When a single
upstream decision reverses and three downstream decisions were built on it, the misaligned
engineering work alone can represent hundreds of thousands of dollars in waste.
3. Proposed Solution
3.1 What MeetingDNA Builds
MeetingDNA is not a meeting summarizer. It is not RAG over transcripts. It is a structured
decision knowledge graph — where every node represents a decision with five properties:
what was decided, who owns it, why it was made, its current status, and which other decisions
depend on it.

4.3 Why This Stack Is Novel
Most hackathon projects follow a predictable pattern:
Data → Embedding → Vector DB → RAG → Chat UI
MeetingDNA's pipeline is architecturally distinct:
Transcripts → LLM Extraction → Structured Decision Nodes
→ Graph DB → Relational + Semantic Query → Agent Response
The three layers that make this genuinely novel are:
• Relation Extraction: Relation extraction — pulling owner, rationale, status, and
dependency links from unstructured conversation is a non-trivial NLP task that most teams
will not attempt
• Graph Traversal: Graph traversal over decisions — querying 'what broke when this
decision reversed' requires graph logic, not vector similarity. This is architecturally distinct
from RAG
• Temporal Reasoning: Temporal reasoning — decisions have status that evolves over
time. Tracking that state change across six months of meetings is genuinely hard
